Storyline

If you're interested why my tagline is nerd on a mission, here's my story:

I had somewhat of a rough start at life. School wasn't working out for me, family and financial problems were certainly a thing, and I dropped out of high school very early on. Yet at 17 an epiphany happened... I'd wasted 17 years accepting defeat, and I needed to flip that around and live a life of service. Courage had dethroned my apathy, and that day I began a pretty fun and challenging journey.

It started with books, and lots of them. Philosophy, world religion, psychology, sociology, history and so on. I hurriedly got my GED, enrolled full time in community college, had academic counselors waive my heavy course loads, and transferred to university after a year.

Some of what's happened since: volunteered at and eventually directed a nonprofit supporting refugees in Portland; got my degree in Social Science, focusing on global challenges such as population, poverty, climate, conflict, resources, disease, etc.; advocated for peace in the mideast, travelled alone through the region twice, learned Turkish and taught English in Turkey; interned with UN - HABITAT in Vietnam, worked on a couple US Embassy funded projects, founded an “entrepreneurship for sustainability” program, and cofounded a hacker and maker space.

I'm more than mildly obsessed with global challenges, community building, the antidote and poison of emerging technologies, effectiveness of humanitarian efforts, and the interplay between science & society.

I'm 24 now, moving into a more focused season of life, and my next set of ambitions will require patient practice. Part of that process is learning how to play with computers. Thus, the last 5 months I've been taking accelerated programs (in-person bootcamp and online) in software dev and data analysis. These will be invaluable skills, and the time taken off from work to learn the basics - and the empty wallet that's followed - has been well worth it.

My long term vision is twofold: to play an ambassador-like role between science & society as we move forward into complex times; and leverage 21st century tools to tackle serious challenges, whether in an organization I join or one I found.

The next 2 years I'll focus on my technical skills, establishing a larger network, and paying the bills (hire me?), preferably alongside an ambitious and altruistic team.
